Reflection

Each and every point on the surface of a lit object constantly emits rays of light that spread in all directions. A few of these rays make it into our eyes, which do all the hard work of assembling the rays from many points to generate the image that we see. When you see yourself in the mirror, the light bouncing off you is reflecting back to your eyes.

This is all built from a simple rule: when a ray of light encounters a surface, the angles that the incoming and outgoing light make with the surface are equal.
